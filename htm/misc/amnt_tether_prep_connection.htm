Finalizing the Connection for Molding

  1. Using the Kevlar strand, tie a half hitch knot on the connector side of the solder joints and secure the knot with a drop of super glue.

  2. Tie a second half hitch knot over the connector and secure it with super glue.

  3. The loose end of the Kevlar can be wrapped around the connector and glued in place.
  4. Slide the 2" heat shrink (0.8 for Neutral tether, 0.4 for negative and PPT) over the wires. Do not cover over the connector.

  5. Wrap at least 1" of aluminum tape around the tether to avoid melting and damaging the tether jacket.
  6. Apply heat to shrink the tubing.

  7. After applying heat, remove the aluminum tape.
  8. Set the connector assembly in the mold and make an indicator mark for priming.

  9. Aggressively sand the metal connector, heat shrink, and tether that will be molded.
  10. Prime the metal connector with Devcon FL-10 (blue) and let it dry. (do not sand or prime the smooth section)
  11. Prime over top the metal blue primer, heat shrink and tether past the mark with Devcon FL-20 (red) and let it dry.
  12. Test each pin for continuity and for shorts one more time before proceeding
