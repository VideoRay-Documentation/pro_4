Operating in Challenging Conditions

Piloting on a project is significantly more challenging that practicing in a pool. Before attempting projects in open water, it is important that the fundamental skills can be executed instinctively and without hesitation. In most cases, these basic skills will be sufficient to complete a project, but there are several situations that can be expected to be encountered on a regular basis. Additional knowledge and skills may be necessary to help pilots achieve their objectives in this situations.

The following sections offer tips and techniques that go beyond the procedures used for basic control in calm clear water.
